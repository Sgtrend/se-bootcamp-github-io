Seven Sisters Waterfall

The Seven Sisters Waterfall is a breathtaking natural spectacle where seven distinct streams cascade side by side over a towering cliff face, plunging more than 250 meters into the Geirangerfjord below. This majestic waterfall is especially impressive during spring when the snowmelt feeds the streams, creating a thunderous roar and a misty spray that adds magic to the landscape. Local legends tell of seven unmarried sisters dancing gracefully down the mountainside, with a nearby waterfall called the Suitor trying to capture their attention. The site is a favorite among photographers and nature enthusiasts for its dramatic scenery and vibrant rainbows that often appear in the sunlight. Visitors can admire the falls from multiple viewpoints, including boat tours that pass directly beneath the cascades, providing a unique perspective and an unforgettable experience. The surrounding area is rich in biodiversity, with native flora and fauna thriving in the moist environment created by the constant spray.
