Eagle Road

The Eagle Road (Ørnevegen) is a breathtaking mountain road featuring 11 hairpin bends that zigzag steeply up the mountainside, offering spectacular panoramic views of the Geirangerfjord below. Constructed in the early 20th century, this engineering marvel was built to provide safer and more direct access to the fjord's interior. Along the way, travelers encounter cascading waterfalls, towering cliffs, and lush, vibrant vegetation that change with the seasons. In spring and summer, the greenery is vivid, while autumn paints the landscape with fiery hues of red and gold. The dramatic scenery and thrilling drive make the Eagle Road a must-see for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike.

At the road's highest point, Eagle Bend (Ørnesvingen), visitors can pause to soak in breathtaking 360-degree views of the fjord, surrounding mountains, and cascading waterfalls below. This viewpoint is especially popular for photography and quiet reflection. Many visitors choose to hike one of the nearby trails for an immersive experience in Norway’s pristine natural beauty. The road also attracts cyclists who enjoy the challenging climbs and rewarding vistas. Throughout the year, the weather changes dramatically, adding unique character to the journey — from misty mornings to clear blue skies or snowy winters creating a magical winter wonderland.
