Geirangerfjord

Geirangerfjord is one of Norway's most iconic natural landmarks, located in the western part of the country. Carved by glaciers during the last Ice Age, it is known for its deep blue waters and dramatic cliffs. The fjord is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular destination for nature photographers. Visitors are often amazed by the stunning waterfalls, winding mountain roads, and peaceful surroundings.
